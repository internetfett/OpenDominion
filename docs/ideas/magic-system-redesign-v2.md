# The Arcane Confluence — Magic System Redesign v2

---

## Design Vision

Magic rewards different skills than military: preparation, information, sustained pressure, and smart resource management. A committed mage should threaten players who would crush them militarily — not through a parallel arms race, but through fundamentally different tools. Defense is an active posture, not a waiting room. Every player in a realm contributes to its magical identity.

---

## Core Vocabulary

**Spell Power (SP)** — Offensive magical capability. Determines spell success rate and potency. Built from Wizards and Spires investment.

**Ward Rating (WR)** — Defensive magical capability. Mitigates the damage and duration of spells that successfully land. Built from Archmages and Wizard Guilds. Does not affect whether spells succeed — only how hard they hit.

**Mana** — Casting fuel. Produced by Towers, capped at a maximum reservoir. No decay.

**Surge Charge** — Stored magical potential generated when the mana reservoir is full. Maximum 5. Spent to Empower spells for amplified effects.

**Ward Charge** — Pre-loaded defensive capacity. Generated passively by Archmages and Wizard Guilds. Consumed automatically by the Stance system when hostile spells land.

**Arcane Overload** — A temporary reduction to Spell Power suffered when offensive spells fail. Recovers passively. Replaces permanent wizard death on failure.

**School Attunement** — A dominion's affinity for each of the four magical schools. Scales from Novice to Grandmaster. Determines effectiveness within each school.

**Mastery Path** — A one-time choice (by Day 7) that defines the player's magical identity for the round. Grants Grandmaster attunement in a school, unique spells, and a passive Realm Aura.

**Magical Stance** — The pre-configured automatic response to incoming hostile magic. Triggers immediately when a spell lands, regardless of whether the player is online.

**Hostile Act** — Individual credit earned against a specific target, granting access to war spells without a realm-level war declaration.

**Blight Stack** — A stackable debuff applied by School of Ruin spells. Maximum 3 per type. Decays 1 per tick.

**Persistent Ward** — A live-updating intelligence link to a target, generated by Empowered Divination spells. Updates every tick for its duration.

**Arcane Saturation** — A protection mechanic that activates when a single dominion receives sustained magical assault from the same source realm. Provides escalating resistance and a temporary immunity cap.

## Resources

### Mana Reservoir

Mana is produced by Towers each tick. It fills a reservoir capped at approximately 12 hours of production. There is no decay.

When the reservoir reaches maximum capacity, surplus production generates **Surge Charges** instead, up to a maximum of 5. Surge Charges do not expire.

**Spending Mana:** Spells cost mana scaled by total land size. Spending empties the reservoir, making room for continued production.

**Empowering with Surge Charges:** Any spell can be Empowered by spending 1 Surge Charge at cast time:
- Self-buff spells: +75% duration
- School of Ruin spells: applies +1 bonus Blight Stack on success
- School of Devastation spells: +25% damage, ignores 25% of target's WR mitigation
- Divination spells: creates a Persistent Ward instead of a snapshot

A player who casts frequently maintains throughput with no Surge Charges. A patient player accumulates Surge Charges for decisive moments. Both are valid.

---

### Ward Charges

Ward Charges are generated passively by Archmages and Wizard Guilds at a combined rate of 1 per hour per threshold of Ward Rating, up to a pool maximum determined by total WR investment.

Ward Charges are consumed automatically by the Stance system when hostile spells land. If the pool is depleted, the active stance falls back to Absorb until charges regenerate.

Ward Charges represent pre-loaded defensive capacity. Building a large pool requires sustained investment in Archmages and Wizard Guilds — the same investment that provides WR mitigation. Defense is an infrastructure decision, not a reactionary one.

## Magical Statistics

### Spell Power

**Sources:** Wizards (primary), Spires castle improvement, offensive tech perks, offensive race perks, Mastery Path bonuses (War Mage, Hexblade).

**Effect:** Determines offensive spell success probability against a fixed difficulty baseline (see below). Higher SP increases success rate toward the hard cap.

### Ward Rating

**Sources:** Archmages (primary, 1.5 WR per archmage / total land), Wizard Guilds (scales with building %), defensive tech perks, defensive race perks, Mastery Path bonuses (Warden).

**Effect:** Reduces the damage and duration of hostile and war spells that successfully land. Does not affect whether spells succeed.

| Ward Rating Level | Blight Stack Duration | War Spell Damage |
|---|---|---|
| Minimal | Full duration | 100% |
| Modest | −20% | 75% |
| Heavy | −40% | 50% |
| Full commitment | −55% | 35% |

The mitigation curve is efficient at modest investment — a player who puts reasonable resources into WR cuts incoming damage roughly in half without needing to match the attacker's commitment.

### Archmages Redesigned

Archmages no longer contribute to Spell Power. They are the dedicated defensive unit, generating Ward Rating and Ward Charges. Their training cost is adjusted downward to reflect this focused role.

## Offensive Spell Success

All hostile, war, and info spells require a success roll. **Success is determined by the caster's Spell Power against a fixed baseline difficulty — the target's Ward Rating does not appear in this formula.** A defender cannot make themselves harder to hit; they can only reduce the damage when hit.

| Spell Power Level | Hostile/Info Success | War Spell Success |
|---|---|---|
| Minimal investment | 35% | 25% |
| Moderate investment | 55% | 45% |
| Heavy investment | 70% | 60% |
| Full commitment | **78% cap** | **68% cap** |

Empowering with a Surge Charge adds +10% to the success roll for that cast.

The hard cap ensures that full commitment to offense is rewarded with consistency, not invincibility. A fully committed attacker succeeds on roughly 3 in 4 hostile casts — meaningful, reliable, and still counterable through Stances and Ward Charge investment.

Info spells (School of the Unseen) use a slightly easier curve than hostile spells, reaching the 78% cap at lower SP investment. Intelligence gathering is accessible to non-specialists.

---

## Arcane Overload

When an offensive spell fails, the caster suffers **Arcane Overload** — a temporary reduction to their Spell Power. No wizards die.

| Mismatch Severity | SP Penalty | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Modest (SP near baseline) | −10% | 4 ticks |
| Moderate | −25% | 6 ticks |
| Heavy (SP well below baseline) | −40% | 8 ticks |

Multiple failed casts stack Overload penalties. Spending a Surge Charge on a failed cast negates the Overload entirely — stored power insulates the caster.

Overload recovers passively each tick. Resilience (generated by being under magical pressure) accelerates recovery. A player who overextends magically is shut down temporarily, not permanently.

## The Magical Stance System

The Stance system replaces reactive counterspelling entirely. Rather than responding to incoming spells after the fact, players configure their defensive posture in advance. Stances trigger **automatically and immediately** when a hostile or war spell lands, regardless of whether the player is online.

### Available Stances

| Stance | Effect | Ward Charge Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Absorb** | Accept the full effect; generate mana equal to 15% of the spell's cost | 0 |
| **Diminish** | Reduce effect potency and duration by 40% | 1 |
| **Dispel** | Remove the effect entirely | 3 |

Absorb is always available and requires no Ward Charges. It is the fallback when the Ward Charge pool is depleted.

Dispel consumes Ward Charges quickly. Against a sustained assault, Ward Charges will eventually run dry and the stance degrades to Absorb automatically. Managing Ward Charge reserves is the core defensive resource decision.

### Active Override

When a player is logged in at the moment a spell lands, a notification fires immediately in the UI with a **60-second window** to override the pre-set stance response for that specific spell. Override options include all three stance tiers plus **Resist** — an active-only option:

**Resist:** Reduces effect by 60% and recovers 1 Ward Charge (net cost: 2 Ward Charges). The best damage-mitigation ratio, but requires a situational judgment call an automated stance cannot make.

If the window expires without action, the pre-set stance fires normally.

Active players are rewarded with better outcomes, not with basic functionality. The offline player's wards still work.

### The Login Log

When a player returns after being offline, the Op Center displays a clear narrative of everything that occurred:

> **Magical Activity (last 11 hours)**
>
> Day 14, 02:18 — Plague landed. **Diminish Stance** activated. Population Growth Blight applied at 60% potency. (−1 Ward Charge)
>
> Day 14, 06:44 — Plague landed. **Diminish Stance** activated. Population Growth Blight renewed at 60% potency. (−1 Ward Charge)
>
> Day 14, 09:31 — Earthquake landed. **Absorb (fallback — Ward Charges depleted).** Earth Blight applied at full potency. (+12 mana generated)
>
> Ward Charges: 0 / 12. Regenerating at 1/hour.

The story is clear: wards held twice, then ran dry. The player understands what happened, why, and what to do differently.

## The Four Schools of Magic

Spells belong to one of four schools. School Attunement — determined by race, buildings, tech, and Mastery Path — scales from Novice (0.7× effectiveness) through Adept (1.0×), Master (1.3×), and Grandmaster (1.6×). Only a Mastery Path grants Grandmaster.

### School of Ruin (Hostile Debuffs)

Entropy and decay. The patient mage who grinds down enemies through sustained pressure.

Ruin spells apply **Blight Stacks** — stackable debuffs that cap at 3 per type and decay 1 per tick. One cast applies 1 stack. Maintaining maximum stacks requires casting once per tick minimum. Different Blight types stack independently.

| Blight Type | Applied By | Effect Per Stack |
|---|---|---|
| Population Growth Blight | Plague | −15% population growth |
| Harvest Blight | Insect Swarm | −12% food production |
| Earth Blight | Earthquake | −12% ore and gem production |
| Maritime Blight | Tidecurse | −15% Dock output and boat generation |
| Strength Blight | Wither | −8% target's Spell Power |

**Attunement effects:**
- Novice: Blight Stacks applied by this caster decay 1 extra tick per tick (unsustainable pressure)
- Adept: Standard decay
- Master: Stacks applied have +1 tick before first decay
- Grandmaster: +2 tick delay before first decay; Empowered casts have 25% chance to apply 2 stacks instead of 1

---

### School of Devastation (War Magic)

Direct damage. The war mage who breaks things when it counts.

Devastation spells require **Hostile Act credit** against the target, or active escalated war. A Hostile Act is earned individually (not realm-wide) when:
- The caster's realm has invaded the target within the last 12 hours
- The target has landed a Ruin or Devastation spell on the caster within the last 24 hours
- The target has stolen resources from the caster within the last 12 hours
- Active escalated war exists between realms

| Spell | Effect |
|---|---|
| Fireball | Destroys unprotected peasants and a portion of food stockpile |
| Lightning Bolt | Destroys castle improvement investment in a player-chosen category (science, keep, forges, or walls) |
| Scorch | Applies Scorched Earth: −20% food production for 6 hours. Cheaper; does not stack with itself |
| Cyclone | Damages wonders. +30% damage during mutual escalated war |

**Critical success** (1.5× damage) is only available to Grandmaster Devastation casters via the Empower mechanic. Variance on war spells is not random for anyone else.

**Attunement effects:**
- Novice: −25% damage, +25% mana cost
- Adept: Standard
- Master: +15% damage; successful casts have a chance to apply Scorched Earth
- Grandmaster: +30% damage; Empowered casts ignore 30% of WR mitigation; 20% critical strike chance on Empowered casts

---

### School of the Unseen (Divination)

Knowledge and intelligence. The mage who knows before acting.

Standard Divination casts produce snapshots. **Empowered casts** (1 Surge Charge) produce **Persistent Wards** — live-updating intelligence that refreshes every tick for 12 hours. A realm with active Persistent Wards has genuinely actionable intelligence: current military, active spells, incoming land, and Blight Stack counts on monitored targets.

| Spell | Normal | Empowered |
|---|---|---|
| Clear Sight | Status snapshot | Persistent Ward: live status |
| Vision | Tech snapshot | Persistent Ward: live tech |
| Revelation | Active spell snapshot | Persistent Ward: live spells |
| Disclosure | Heroes snapshot | Persistent Ward: live heroes |
| Omen Reading | Reveals Blight Stacks on target (count and type) | Master+: also reveals source caster |

**Attunement effects:**
- Novice: Persistent Wards not accessible (Empower produces extended snapshots instead)
- Adept: Standard Persistent Wards (12h, updates per tick)
- Master: Persistent Wards last 18h; Omen Reading reveals source caster
- Grandmaster: Wards update every 30 minutes; also reveals target's Surge Charge count and open stance

---

### School of Warding (Protection)

Defense and resilience. Available to all players for self-cast spells; role-restricted for ally-targeted versions.

**Self-cast (always succeed, no roll):**

| Spell | Duration | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Energy Mirror | 12h | +20% WR (Novice: +10%; Master: lasts 18h) |
| Mana Veil | 12h | Identity hidden from Surreal Perception while active |
| Arcane Reserve | Instant | Converts 25% of current mana into 1 Surge Charge |
| Surreal Perception | 12h | Reveals source of all successful ops and spells against you |
| Fool's Gold | 10h | Protects platinum from theft; tech upgrade extends to ore, lumber, and mana |

**Ally-targeted (Grand Magister / Court Mage only):**

| Spell | Duration | Effect | Cooldown |
|---|---|---|---|
| Arcane Ward | 6h | +25% WR on realmmate (Master: +35%) | 8h per target |
| Ley Anchor | 6h | +15% WR and +15% spy defense on realmmate | 8h per target |
| Sanctum Shield | 6h | Negates the next hostile or war spell on realmmate entirely | Refreshable before consumed |

Sanctum Shield provides full negation, not reflection. The Grandmaster Warden path adds a 25% power reflection — the only reflection in the system, non-amplified.

## Mastery Paths

By Day 7 of the round, each player chooses one of five Mastery Paths. This choice is permanent. Each path provides Grandmaster attunement in one school, access to exclusive spells, and a **Realm Aura** — a passive bonus applied to all realm members simultaneously, unconditionally, with no title required.

---

### The Arcanist

*Breadth over depth. The realm's generalist and logistics engine.*

**Attunement:** Adept in all four schools.

**Exclusive Spells:**
- **Ley Shift** — Transfer up to 25% of current mana to a realmmate. Once per 24 hours.
- **Arcane Audit** — Reveals target's School Attunement levels, Mastery Path, and Surge Charge count. Costs 1 Surge Charge.

**Realm Aura:** All realm members receive 5–8% reduction to all spell mana costs (scales with Arcanist's Tower count).

---

### The War Mage

*The realm's hammer. Precision destruction when it matters most.*

**Attunement:** Grandmaster in School of Devastation. Novice in School of Ruin. Adept in others.

**Exclusive Spells:**
- **Inferno** — 2× Fireball damage, ignores Wizard Guild protection entirely, applies Scorched Earth. Once per 72 hours. Extremely expensive.
- **Siege Mark** — All Devastation spells cast by any realm member against the marked target deal +15% damage for 12 hours. One active mark at a time.

**Realm Aura:** During active escalated war, all realm members gain +5% offensive military power.

---

### The Hexblade

*Sustained attrition. The mage who makes enemies defeat themselves.*

**Attunement:** Grandmaster in School of Ruin. Novice in School of Devastation. Adept in others.

**Exclusive Spells:**
- **Withering Pall** — Doubles all current Blight Stacks of every active type on the target (reset decay clock on all affected stacks). Requires the target to have at least 1 Blight Stack. Costs 2 Surge Charges.
- **Hex Chain** — The next School of Ruin spell cast on the target by any realm member applies +1 bonus stack. Lasts 12 hours or until triggered.

**Realm Aura:** All Blight Stacks applied by realm members decay 1 tick later than normal.

---

### The Seer

*Information dominance. The mage whose knowledge wins battles before they start.*

**Attunement:** Grandmaster in School of the Unseen. Novice in School of Devastation. Adept in others.

**Exclusive Spells:**
- **Fate Sight** — Applies an Empowered Clear Sight (Persistent Ward) to all dominions in a target realm simultaneously. Single cast, significant mana cost.
- **Reveal Weakness** — Reveals the exact offensive or defensive power a target would field in a hypothetical engagement, including all active bonuses. Once per target per day.

**Realm Aura:** All realm members' Persistent Wards update every 30 minutes instead of per tick.

---

### The Warden

*The realm's shield. Makes everyone harder to break.*

**Attunement:** Grandmaster in School of Warding. Novice in School of Ruin. Adept in others.

**Exclusive Spells:**
- **Grand Sanctum** — Applies Sanctum Shield to all realm members simultaneously. Duration: 3 hours. Costs 3 Surge Charges.
- **Arcane Retribution** — Grants a specific realm member active-only Resist access for their next incoming spell, regardless of their Ward Rating. No Ward Charges required for that use.

**Realm Aura:** All realm members have their Diminish Stance effectiveness increased to 50% reduction (up from 40%). The entire realm pushes back harder against hostile magic.

## Arcane Saturation

Arcane Saturation tracks the total number of hostile and war spells a dominion has received from a single source realm within a rolling 24-hour window. It provides escalating resistance and a hard immunity cap against sustained griefing.

| Saturation | Threshold | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| None | 0–3 spells | No change |
| Low | 4–6 spells | Diminish Stance costs 1 fewer Ward Charge from that realm's spells |
| Medium | 7–10 spells | Incoming Blight Stacks from that realm decay 1 extra tick per tick |
| High | 11–14 spells | Blight Stacks decay 2 extra ticks; war spell damage reduced by 20% |
| **Arcane Immunity** | 15+ spells | All hostile and war spells from that realm automatically fail for 12 hours. No Overload applied to casters. After 12 hours, Saturation resets to zero. |

Saturation is tracked per source realm, not per individual caster. Rotating which realm members cast does not reset or circumvent accumulation.

Arcane Immunity is a hard stop, not a soft one. A realm can conduct sustained magical campaigns — they simply cannot do so uninterrupted forever. The natural rhythm becomes: assault phase, forced recovery window, next assault phase. The victim can plan around it.

## The Arcane Congress

Once per 72 hours, the Grand Magister (or any Mastery Path holder) may call an Arcane Congress. All realm members are notified and have a 4-hour window to contribute mana to the shared pool. When the window closes, the caller selects from available Congress Spells based on the total contributed.

| Pool Required | Congress Spell | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 500+ | **Ley Blessing** | All realm members +15% mana production for 24 hours |
| 1,500+ | **Arcane Bulwark** | All realm members +20% WR for 24 hours |
| 3,000+ | **Siege Confluence** | All Devastation spells against a designated target +30% damage for 12 hours (requires Hostile Acts) |
| 6,000+ | **Grand Warding** | Applies Sanctum Shield and Arcane Ward to every realm member simultaneously, 6 hours |
| 10,000+ | **Apocalypse Confluence** | Combined Fireball and Lightning Bolt strike on a designated target: 5× standard damage each, ignores 50% WR. Requires mutual escalated war. Once per round per realm. |

Any realm member can contribute mana regardless of title or Mastery Path. The Congress is the mechanical expression of the realm's collective magical investment.

## Racial Attunement

Each race has a preset School Attunement profile reflecting their magical identity. Races built around magic begin with higher attunements; military and economic races begin at Novice in most schools. Buildings, tech, and Mastery Path can improve attunement from there, but only a Mastery Path reaches Grandmaster.

**Notable racial adjustments from the current system:**

**Immortal Wizards** perk is renamed **Resolute Archmages** and redesigned: Archmages of this race generate +50% Ward Rating per unit. Since wizards no longer die on failure (replaced by Arcane Overload), the original perk's function is obsolete; this version provides meaningful defensive identity instead.

**Unholy Ghost (Dark Elf / Spirit)** is reclassified as a School of Ruin effect: applies Draftee Blight (enemy draftees contribute 50% DP instead of 100% during invasions). Functions as a single non-stackable Blight that lasts 10 hours. Preserved power, repositioned as sustained-investment magic.

**Erosion (Merfolk / Lizardfolk)** is now a targeted application: each cast rezones up to 50% of recently conquered acres to water. The player selects which acres. No more involuntary terrain transformation of land you wanted.

**Gaia's Light / Gaia's Shadow (Wood Elf)** are no longer mutually penalizing. Gaia's Light grants a Spell Power bonus; Gaia's Shadow grants a spy power bonus. Neither penalizes the other stat. The choice is which to boost, not which penalty to accept.

**Death and Decay (Undead)** has two explicit cast modes with separate confirmation prompts: Ruin Mode (applies Blight Stacks to a target using the caster's Ruin attunement) and Internal Mode (accelerates food decay and converts peasants to zombies). Internal Mode requires deliberate selection. It cannot be cast accidentally.

## Player Decision Space

**Mana Reservoir vs. Surge Charge timing.** Cast frequently to maintain throughput and sustained pressure. Cast patiently to bank Surge Charges for decisive Empowered strikes, Congress contributions, or Ward-building. Both patterns are viable; most players will do some of both.

**Spell Power vs. Ward Rating allocation.** Full commitment to SP yields consistent ~78% success rates but leaves WR near zero — every spell that hits will hit hard. Full commitment to WR cuts incoming damage by 55% but your own offensive output is weak. The question of where to sit on this axis is the central magical identity decision.

**Ward Charge reserve management.** Running Dispel Stance depletes Ward Charges quickly. Sustaining Dispel against a Hexblade campaign for 24 hours may require more Ward Charge generation than most builds can maintain — forcing a fall to Diminish or Absorb. Knowing when to spend charges and when to let hits through is the core defensive skill.

**Stance pre-configuration before logging off.** What magical threats are likely while offline? Is the realm at war? Is a Hexblade known to be targeting the dominion? Setting the right stance before going offline is a meaningful preparation decision with real consequences.

**Mastery Path as realm coordination.** The Warden's Realm Aura benefits every teammate. The Seer's Fate Sight serves the realm's military attackers. The War Mage's Siege Mark amplifies every other Devastation caster in the realm. Mastery Path choices have the most value when they complement each other — a realm that talks through its Path selections before Day 7 will outperform one that doesn't.

**Arcane Congress pool targeting.** Call a Congress early for the Ley Blessing (low cost, sustainable), or wait for enough mana to unlock Siege Confluence or Grand Warding? Hold the once-per-round Apocalypse Confluence for a war-deciding moment, or spend it opportunistically? The 72-hour cooldown makes every Congress call a real decision.

**Hostile Acts as individual diplomatic texture.** War spells don't require a realm-level war declaration — just earned credit against a specific target. Being attacked magically grants retaliatory Devastation access. Players develop individual Hostile Act histories with specific enemies, independent of their realm's diplomatic state. A Hexblade who has been harassing you just handed you Fireball access against them.
